Sleep in late
You currently have a 91 in your art history class, but you were hoping to receive an A, as this was an easy gen ed to knock out. Your professor gives an opportunity for extra credit if you visit the Ackland Art Museum and write a two-page paper on it. You have been so busy all week and have had no time to yourself to rest. Do you take your Saturday to do the extra credit, or do you make the day a self-care day and relax?
Do extra credit
Take self-care day
You have a two-hour window between your classes in an otherwise busy day. Do you use the time to lift at the Student Rec Center or use the time to get Starbucks and get ahead on an Econ assignment?
Go to the SRC
Go to Starbucks
You get to Starbucks and see a friend you've been meaning to catch up with. Do you sit with them or sit alone?
Sit alone
Sit together
Your final for your statistics class is tomorrow at 10:30 am. Although you feel mostly prepared, there are still some concepts that you struggle with. Do you go to sleep early, assuming that you know enough to get a decent grade, or do you stay up super late cramming?
Go to sleep now
Cram more studying
You wake up for class feeling achy and are coughing a lot. Your 8 am is very strict on attendance and gives a 1-point penalty for any absence, excused or not. You have already missed class twice. Will you go to class?
Go to class
Rest up
You get to class, and your professor is giving a very important lecture. You start feeling even worse to the point you can barely focus. Do you stay for the important lecture or excuse yourself and go to Campus Health?
Stay for lecture
Go to Campus Health
It's the last day of class in the semester, and you plan on driving home tomorrow to hang out with your high school friends who you haven’t seen in a while. However, a service club that you hold leadership in reschedules its meeting to the same day you plan on going home. Do you stay for the meeting and miss the event with your friends or go home and miss the meeting?
Go to meeting
Go home
You’re planning your classes for next semester. You have the option to stick to 15 credits and stay on pace with your degree plan, or add another 3-credit class and knock out your last gen ed, lightening your course load for junior and senior year. Do you add the gen ed?
Add gen ed
Don't add it
You have extra time in your schedule since you only took 15 credits. Do you commit your time to another club or start consistently going to the gym?
Gym
Club
You're living on your own next semester, and your parents will contribute toward your food budgeting. You can either get an on campus meal plan, allowing you to socialize more at the dining halls, or you can use the money to buy groceries and cook your own healthy meals at home. Do you get the meal plan, risking eating unhealthier, or make meals with fresh ingredients, risking socialization with your friends?
Meal plan
Buy groceries
You found out through your LFIT that you are really good at ultimate frisbee. You have some extra time in your schedule. Do you join the club team or utilize the CAPS resources to help with your anxiety?
Use CAPS resources
Join ultimate frisbee
Good news! An artist you really like is coming to Raleigh, and tickets are cheap. However, the concert is at the same time as your sorority philanthropy night. Which one are you going to?
Go to the concert
Go to sorority event
